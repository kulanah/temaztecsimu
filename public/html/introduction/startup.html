Startup


Pre-Start Checklist

To begin, we will have to click “Microscope Setup” button to bring up the Microscope Setup Window – this is where we’ll see the fluorescence screen output.

Click the “Microscope Controls” button to view the two control panels and the aperture knobs.

Click the yellow “Col. Valves Closed” button within the simulated TEM User Interface software to open the column valves.

Click the left control panel’s “L3” button twice – this will change the spot size setting from 5 to 3.

Click, hold, and drag the left hand control panel’s “beam shift” trackball to (roughly) center the beam over the large black circle within the microscope setup window.

Click the “eucentric focus” button on the right hand control panel to optimize the objective lens current.

Click and drag the “magnification” knob until the displayed magnification reads: “34000 x”.

Use the “beam shift trackball” to re-center the beam over the central circles.

Use the left hand control panel’s “intensity” knob to converge the beam to a small point, and verify that this point is within the Microscope Setup Window’s innermost black circle.

If it is not, correct the positioning using the “beam shift” trackball.

Click and drag the “magnification” knob until the displayed magnification reads: “125k x”.

Re-center the beam with the “beam shift” trackball if necessary.

Use the “intensity” knob to diverge the beam until it is only slightly larger than the inner black circle.

Inspect its shape to ensure that it is as circular as possible.

If it is not circular, click the “Stigmator” button on the left hand control panel.


Condenser Stigmation

Within the new “Stigmator” popup window, click the “Condenser” button.

Carefully adjust the two “multi-function” knobs on the left and right control panels until the beam is as circular as you can manage.

Click “None” within the stigmator popup window to finish the adjustment.

Click the “Stigmator” button on the left hand control panel to close the popup window.
