TEM Syllabus


Course Goals:

The course goal is for the students to become comptent, research-level transmission electron microscopists. They will understand the functions of the TEM and how it works. They will be competent in basic operating techniques, and ready to learn more advanced ones as needed.

Course Content:

I. Transmission Electron Microscope

  1. Introduction to transmission electron microscopy
  2. Electron source working principle and characterization
  3. Electron optics and TEM alignment
  4. Resolution and lens aberration
  5. Vacuum system and maintenance
  6. Electrons and specimen interaction
  7. Electron detectors and digital imaging

II. Electron Diffraction

  1. Introduction to crystallography
  2. Reciprocal space and Ewald reconstruction
  3. Stereo projection
  4. Electron diffraction—kinematic theory
  5. Electron diffraction—dynamic theory
  6. Shape factors and the deviation parameter
  7. SAED patterns and applications
  8. Kikuchi diffraction patterns and applications
  9. Convergent-beam electron diffraction (CBED) and applications
  10. Double diffraction, twins, polycrystalline materials, amorphous materials

III. Imaging

  1. Contrast mechanisms in TEM/STEM
  2. Defect analysis with TEM imaging
  3. High resolution imaging: Phase contrast
  4. Scanning transmission electron microscopy and Z contrast imaging
  5. Moiré, Lorentz, and holographic imaging
  6. Digital image processing

IV. Microanalysis

  1. Inelastic electronic scatterings and microanalysis
  2. Introduction to energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy
  3. Qualitative and quantitative analysis with EDX
  4. Introduction to electron energy-loss spectroscopy (EELS)
  5. Energy-filtered imaging and energy-filtered diffraction
  6. Electron-beam-induced specimen damage and contamination
